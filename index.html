Billionaires Across the Globe

The visualization displays the number of billionaires in each country. With 724 billionaires, the United States continues to lead the world in this regard (up from 614 last year). China is bridging the gap with 626 (excluding 71 from Hong Kong and 1 from Macao). Beijing now has more billionaires than any other city in the world, surpassing New York City, thanks to China's economic growth. With 140 billionaires, India is the third-highest billionaire country. The combined wealth of the 1,149 billionaires from the Asia-Pacific region is $4.7 trillion, whereas the combined wealth of American billionaires is $4.4 trillion.

Most Prevalent Industries

With 371 billionaires, or 13% of the total list, Finance and Investments is without a doubt the industry that has created the most billionaires globally. At 365, the Technology sector comes in second, with Jeff Bezos at the top of the list. This year, the Manufacturing industry ranked third with 331 billionaires, 155 of whom were Chinese. Despite the restrictions, the Fashion and Retail industries placed fourth overall with 273 billionaires, or 10% of the list for this year. Here you'll find some of the most illustrious names in fashion, like Chanel's controllers Alain and Gerard Wertheimer and LVMH CEO Bernard Arnault.

Age Range

The majority of billionaires belong to an older age group, which is especially true given that 96% of all billionaires worldwide are over 40 and 32% are over 70. In spite of the fact that the total number of billionaires climbed by a net 660 to 2,755, there are still only 10 billionaires in the world who are under the age of 30. The title of the world's youngest billionaire belongs to Kevin David Lehmann, who was just 18 when he inherited his father's 50% stake in a German drugstore chain.
